Why Gold Prices Change Daily

Gold prices fluctuate due to several global and local economic factors. Understanding these factors can help you better predict market trends.

  • International Market Prices – Gold is traded globally and international demand directly affects prices.
  • Currency Exchange Rate – When the Indian Rupee weakens against the US Dollar, gold prices increase.
  • Inflation – Investors buy gold to protect their wealth during inflation.
  • Government Policies – Import duties and taxes can impact gold prices in India.
  • Seasonal Demand – Festivals and wedding seasons increase demand for gold.

24K vs 22K Gold – What's the Difference?

When checking the gold price today, you will usually see rates for both 24K gold and 22K gold.

24K Gold

24 Karat gold is the purest form of gold with around 99.9% purity. It is mainly used for gold coins and investment bars.

22K Gold

22 Karat gold contains about 91.6% gold mixed with small amounts of other metals like copper or silver. This makes it stronger and ideal for jewelry.

Is Gold a Good Investment?

Gold has always been considered a safe investment during uncertain economic conditions. Unlike many other assets, gold usually retains its value over long periods of time.

Benefits of investing in gold include:

  • Protection against inflation
  • High liquidity
  • Global demand
  • Long-term value preservation

Investors can buy gold in multiple forms including jewelry, gold coins, gold bars, ETFs, and digital gold.
